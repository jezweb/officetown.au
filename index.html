Your AI's memory, in plain sight.

Most AI assistants forget you the moment you close the tab — and what they do remember is locked in a black box. Office Town is a personal cortex that remembers your work, your people, your decisions as plain markdown files on your own Cloudflare. You can open every one in Finder. It gives your AI seven real capabilities — memory, files, email, cron, voice, code, and interactive apps — plus a team of named specialists. Empty your filing cabinet into it and watch it learn your business.

What it feels like to use

A few real interactions you'd have after installing. Each one persists — the next session knows what happened in the last.

@librarian
"Extract everything I know about Acme Corp — emails I've mentioned them in, projects we've done, the people involved."
Pulls the threads, writes a wiki/orgs/acme-corp/entity.md with the people linked to wiki/contacts/, projects linked to wiki/projects/, and an engagement trace of every interaction it found. Future you can just ask "what's the status with Acme?" and it knows.
@boss
"I need to follow up with everyone who got a quote in the last 6 weeks but didn't reply."
Queries the wiki, finds the 12 candidates, drafts personalised follow-up emails. You approve each, it sends via your Cloudflare Email Routing. Schedules a 2-week cron to chase any that still haven't replied.
@worker
"Build a one-page proposal for the Acme job using last month's similar project as a template, then save it as a sharable link."
Reads wiki/projects/last-similar.md, drafts the proposal, generates a PDF, publishes it at /p/acme-proposal-2026-05 on your own domain — no third-party uploads.
@scout
"What's new in Cloudflare Workers this month that's relevant to our hosting work?"
Scans the changelog, blog, and the bits you flagged as relevant in wiki/knowledge/cloudflare-watch.md. Writes a 5-bullet summary, files it in your knowledge collection, and offers to email it to the team.

Apps your agent opens — or builds

Office Town ships interactive apps that run as windows inside Goose Desktop — a task board, a quote-to-cash flow, a mini-CRM, an on-site quoter, a bookings calendar, and more. You click and type directly; they save straight to your cortex. The agent can open one for you, build a brand-new one on request, or share a customer-facing one behind a magic link.

Direct manipulation

Click a field, overtype it, it saves. No “hey AI, change the phone number.”

Agent-authored

Magic-link share

Install in 5 minutes

Three steps. The first two click through a button; the third is a single line in your terminal.

1 Get Goose if you don't have it

Goose is the local agent runtime — free, open source, runs your AI personas. Desktop or CLI, either works.

Get Goose →

2 Deploy your Office Town backend

One click. Cloudflare signs you in (no API token), provisions everything — D1, R2, Vectorize, Queues, Workers AI — and gives you a worker URL plus a bearer token to save.

🚀 Deploy to Cloudflare

Free Cloudflare tier handles ~100K MCP calls/day. Most people never pay.

3 Run one line in your terminal

It installs the Goose CLI + plugin if needed, wires the 6 MCPs, and creates your cortex as a real folder at ~/OfficeTown/ — synced both ways with your Cloudflare.

curl -fsSL https://YOUR-WORKER.workers.dev/connect.sh | \
  WORKER_URL='https://YOUR-WORKER.workers.dev' \
  MCP_BEARER='YOUR_BEARER_TOKEN' bash

After step 2, your dashboard's /dashboard/connect page hands you this pre-filled — copy, paste, done.

✓ Then just say hi

Quit and reopen Goose Desktop so it loads the new extensions. Open a fresh chat and type "hi" — an agent welcomes you and offers a few ways to start. The fastest: drop a pile of your real documents into ~/OfficeTown/inbox/ and ask it to go through them. It'll read everything and learn your business.

The shape

Markdown files on your Cloudflare. Hybrid keyword + semantic search built in. Edit in Obsidian, Goose, the web dashboard, or any text editor — they all talk to the same source of truth.

your-cortex/ ├── wiki/ │ ├── orgs/ # clients, prospects, vendors │ ├── contacts/ # humans you work with │ ├── projects/ # active + historical work │ ├── decisions/ # why we chose X │ ├── knowledge/ # graduated findings │ ├── owner/ # your voice, rhythm, preferences │ ├── team/ # humans + agent profiles │ ├── business/ # who you are │ └── tasks/ # open, in-flight, done ├── files/ # R2-backed file storage └── AGENTS.md # your town's standing orders

The team you get

Four addressable roles, each with a clear scope. Address by name; they coordinate via the shared wiki.

💬 @boss

Talks to you. Routes work. Holds the thread across sessions. Never does deep work itself — delegates and reports back.

📚 @librarian

Extracts knowledge from your sources. Curates the wiki. Graduates findings into durable concepts the rest of the team can rely on.

⚙️ @worker

Deep work. Building. Research. Ships artefacts. Names trade-offs honestly — doesn't gloss over what it's uncertain about.

🔭 @scout

Outward-facing. Industry. Tools. World. Surfaces things the inward-focused roles wouldn't notice. Comes back with what to do about it.

Why this exists

Most AI tools are stateless. Office Town treats memory as a first-class substrate, not an afterthought — your data lives on your Cloudflare, in markdown, forever editable, with the AI as one access pattern among several.

Your data, your account

No third-party SaaS. The wiki + files + cron all live on your own Cloudflare account. Free tier covers ~100K calls/day.

Markdown as substrate

Every entry is a plain markdown file with YAML frontmatter. Editable in Obsidian, VSCode, Typora — anywhere. The AI just reads + writes the same files.

Hybrid search built in

FTS5 keyword + Vectorize semantic search. Find by exact term or by meaning — both surface the same kinds of insight.

Audit trail by default

Every wiki write requires a why: — the reason for the change. You can answer "who changed what, when, and why" without grep-ing logs.

Composable, not monolithic

7 MCP servers + 4 roles + opt-in app & role packs. Start with the backend; layer apps and agents on top as the work demands.

Generative interfaces

Apps are markdown-and-HTML the agent can write on demand — a real UI for a single moment or a single customer, backed by your own cortex, not a rigid SaaS.
